Base

Operating System/Distribution
I use Arch Linux; I made the switch in early 2024 after I moved from a desktop oriented workflow to predominantly working on an X220. It’s not as minimal as Gentoo; however, when your machine’s weak it’s a hell of a lot harder to rationalize building your system from source. Arch Linux seems to be the best middle ground between minimalism and quick maintenance.
Terminal
I’ve been using st since I moved away from Mint. To my knowledge, it’s the most minimal terminal you can get away with using. The beauty of Suckless software is the accessibility of their source code; chances are if there’s something you’re missing in the base install you can find a patch, or even better, write one yourself.
Shell
I’m not sure why I use Zsh ; it’s got all of the features of batch and plugin support for auto-completion and syntax colouring. Despite not being able to give a reason to why I use it I can recommend it on the basis that I’ve never ran into a problem with it.
Window Manager
dwm is the sole reason I moved away from Mint; I felt like I was watching wizards effortlessly snapping between ten different windows, all organized in absurd orientations—while they look neat there’s only a few layouts you need to patch in; I have yet to have wandered into a use case for the fibonacci layout.

I lied, I dual boot Windows

As much as it pains me to admit it, I dual boot Windows 10 on an old hard-drive; this is for a few reasons, mainly my dependence on Adobe’s Creative Cloud suite for work. After nine years staring at After Effects it would be ludicrous to burden my clients with a lengthy readjustment period to Davinci Resolve or another Linux alternative… I’m also bloody cracked at Fortnite.
